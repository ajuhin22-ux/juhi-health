Ox Bile — The Evidence vs. The Opinion

Dr. Kamath said no — but gave no specific medical reasoning. He did not say it creates dependency (that was about triphala).

What the literature says:
  • Post-cholecystectomy patients have significantly lower Vitamin D levels and bone mineral density (PubMed 2018)
  • Without a gall bladder, bile is no longer stored and released on demand — it drips continuously. Large fat-containing meals don't get adequate bile for digestion (Dr. Neil Levin)
  • Ox bile is a digestive enzyme replacement — it replaces what your body no longer produces on demand. It is NOT a stimulant laxative like triphala.
  • Your Vitamin D crashed 44% (45.7 to 25.4) despite supplementation — this pattern is consistent with fat-soluble vitamin malabsorption (IBS Treatment Center)
  • Multiple surgeon-authored guides recommend bile salt supplementation post-cholecystectomy (Dr. Adam Harris, M.D.)
Recommendation: Don't start without a doctor's OK. But bring this evidence to a gastroenterologist (not a general surgeon) for a second opinion specifically on bile supplementation. Dr. Kamath is excellent for surgery — but bile acid management is a GI specialist's domain.
