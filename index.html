The Sneezing

What you're describing — constant sneezing, worsened in last 2-3 months despite air purifier and keeping house dust-free — sounds like allergic rhinitis (hay fever). This is extremely common and your IgE at 83.96 (normal <100) doesn't rule it out — IgE can be normal in localized allergic rhinitis.

Possible triggers:

  • Dust mites — the most common indoor allergen. Air purifiers help but mattress/pillow covers are key
  • Mumbai humidity + mold — check bathroom, AC vents, behind furniture
  • Post-nasal drip from sinusitis — can be linked to your inflammation picture
  • Stress-induced immune dysregulation — your immune system is already overactive (WBC 11,480). Allergic reactions worsen when the immune system is in overdrive.

The Nose Bump

A small, very tiny bump at the tip-right of your nose developing over 2-3 months could be:

  • Nasal vestibular cyst or polyp — benign, related to chronic inflammation/allergies
  • Small sebaceous cyst — common, harmless
  • Nasal vestibulitis — minor infection at nostril entrance from nose-blowing/picking
  • Rhinophyma (very unlikely at your age)
Action needed:

Polyester Clothing & Hormones

Is it real?

Yes, there is growing evidence, but context matters.

A 2024 review of 120+ studies found widespread phthalate contamination in synthetic textiles. Polyester can contain:

  • BPA (Bisphenol A) — estrogen-mimicking chemical that can leach onto skin
  • Phthalates — endocrine disruptors linked to menstrual/ovulatory problems
  • Antimony + PFAS — used in manufacturing, reproductively toxic
  • Microplastics — shed from synthetic clothes and carry hormone-disrupting chemicals

How worried should you be?

The evidence is strongest for underwear and clothing worn directly against skin for long hours (like your H&M black polyester pants you wear daily). The risk is higher with:

  • Tight-fitting synthetic clothes (more skin contact + heat)
  • Sweating in synthetic fabrics (heat opens pores, chemicals leach more)
  • Wearing synthetics in the groin/reproductive area
Practical recommendation (not paranoia, just common sense):
  1. Underwear: Switch to 100% cotton. This is the easiest, most impactful change.
  2. Daily pants: When you can, prefer cotton or cotton-blend trousers over pure polyester. You don't need to throw away all your H&M pants — just don't wear the same synthetic pants every day.
  3. Gym clothes: Synthetic is OK for workouts (you sweat it out and shower after). Just don't live in them.
  4. Sleep clothes: 100% cotton. You spend 7-8 hours in them.
  5. Wash new clothes before first wear — removes some chemical residues

This is NOT the biggest factor in your health picture. Your inflammation, prolactin, stress, and diet are 10x more impactful. But switching to cotton underwear and sleepwear is an easy win.
